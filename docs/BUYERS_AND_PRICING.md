# ChronoScope AI — Buyers & Pricing Strategy

---

## The Core Pricing Principle

ChronoScope saves buyers more than it costs.
Every price below is set at roughly 10-20% of the value delivered.
At that ratio, nobody argues about the price.

---

## Pricing Tiers

### Tier 1 — Pilot License
**$75,000 CAD / year**

For: First customer, proof of concept deployment
Includes:
- Single operations center deployment
- Up to 5 concurrent users
- NOAA/NASA public data sources
- Email support
- Quarterly check-in calls

Use this price to get your first customer.
One paying customer changes every subsequent conversation.

---

### Tier 2 — Operational License
**$150,000 – $250,000 CAD / year**

For: Active satellite operators, government agencies
Includes:
- Single operations center deployment
- Up to 20 concurrent users
- All data sources including custom adapters
- Priority support with 4-hour response
- Monthly check-in calls
- Quarterly platform updates

---

### Tier 3 — Enterprise License
**$500,000 – $1,000,000 CAD / year**

For: Large agencies, multi-mission operators, defense
Includes:
- Unlimited deployment within one organization
- Unlimited users
- Custom data adapter development
- On-site deployment support
- Dedicated support contact
- Source code escrow
- SLA with uptime guarantees

---

### Tier 4 — Government Contract / SBIR
**$150,000 – $1,500,000 CAD**

For: NASA, CSA, DND, allied space commands
Structure:
- Phase I SBIR/IRAP: $150,000 – $300,000
- Phase II SBIR/STTR: $750,000 – $1,500,000
- Sole source contract after Phase II: $500,000+/year

---

### Tier 5 — Acquisition
**$5,000,000 – $50,000,000 CAD**

Timing: After first paying customer + SBIR award
Buyers: Defense primes, aerospace integrators, software platforms
Structure: Asset purchase or share purchase
Retention: 12-24 month earnout tied to customer retention

## The Conversation Sequence

Every buyer conversation follows this sequence:

**Email or LinkedIn message:**
"We built a platform that reduces satellite anomaly investigation
from weeks to hours. It runs live on DSCOVR spacecraft data right
now. Can I show you a 10-minute demo?"

**Demo (10 minutes):**
Run `python scripts/sale_demo.py`
Show the output.
Let the real NASA data speak.

**Follow-up:**
Send the JSON report and Markdown report generated by the demo.
Send the PITCH.md document.
Ask: "What does your current anomaly investigation process look like?"

**Proposal:**
Customize the pitch to their specific pain.
Reference their specific missions or satellites.
Offer pilot terms.
